# QuantatomAI Grid Engine – Competitive Battlecard

## Positioning

QuantatomAI’s Data Grid Engine is the core of an AI-native planning and reporting platform. It delivers all the grid capabilities customers expect from Anaplan, Pigment, Oracle PBCS, Adaptive, and Jedox—and then goes beyond them with atoms, waves, AI, offline resilience, and multi-cloud governance.

## Customer problem

Modern finance and planning teams need:

- Fast, flexible, multidimensional grids.
- Real-time calculations and writeback.
- Rich planning operations (spreads, allocations, scenarios).
- Reliable offline work and conflict handling.
- Trustworthy AI assistance, not black-box magic.
- A platform that scales with data, users, and complexity.

Legacy tools often:

- Lock you into a single vendor cloud.
- Struggle with scale and complexity.
- Treat AI as a bolt-on.
- Ignore offline and conflict realities.
- Hide their calculation logic.

## Competitive snapshot

- **Anaplan:** Strong real-time grid, proprietary engine, single cloud, limited AI-native UX.
- **Pigment:** Modern React grid, strong UX, single engine, limited offline and conflict handling.
- **Oracle PBCS:** Cube-based grids, strong consolidation, heavier UX, limited AI and offline.
- **Adaptive/Jedox:** Spreadsheet-centric, less modern UX, limited AI-native behavior.

**QuantatomAI:**

- Atom-native lattice + Rust SIMD compute (HelioCalc).
- Wave-based QoS for grid operations.
- AI-native UX (AUH, Oracle 2.0).
- Offline-first with ICH conflict harmonization.
- Multi-cloud, ESG-aware, privacy-first.

## Key differentiators

### 1. Atom-native grid

- Every cell is backed by atoms, not cube blocks or spreadsheet cells.
- Better sparsity, better scaling, more flexible modeling.
- Competitors: proprietary engines, but not atom-exposed or programmable.

### 2. Wave QoS for grid operations

- Interactive grids are treated as high-priority waves.
- Heavy allocations and bulk operations are background waves.
- Guarantees planner responsiveness even under load.
- Competitors: no explicit wave QoS fabric.

### 3. Offline + conflict harmonization

- Full offline snapshot and delta log.
- Intelligent Conflict Harmonizer (ICH) detects and explains conflicts.
- AUH guides users through resolution.
- Competitors: limited or no offline, no intelligent conflict UX.

### 4. AI-native UX

- AUH suggests grid layouts, filters, groupings, and KPIs.
- Oracle 2.0 explains variances and suggests next views.
- AI is governed (budgets, policies, privacy), not a black box.
- Competitors: AI as a side feature, not embedded in grid behavior.

### 5. ESG and privacy by design

- GRB can make heavy grid jobs carbon-aware (opt-in).
- PEV ensures sensitive data is encrypted and masked appropriately.
- MCGF provides cross-cloud audit trails.
- Competitors: limited or no ESG-aware compute, less transparent privacy controls.

### 6. Programmable grids

- Grids are first-class, versioned, API-addressable objects.
- Developers can build apps, workflows, and VizBoards on top of grid queries.
- Competitors: grids are mostly UI constructs, not programmable substrates.

## Objection handling

- **“We already use Anaplan/Pigment.”**  
  QuantatomAI can coexist, ingest, and reconcile their models via LRI, while offering a more open, AI-native, multi-cloud future.

- **“We’re worried about AI reliability.”**  
  AI is governed: budgets, policies, fallbacks, and explainability are built-in. Core grid behavior does not depend on AI availability.

- **“We need Excel parity.”**  
  Keyboard shortcuts, copy/paste, spreads, allocations, and Smart View-like behavior are all supported—plus more modern UX and AI guidance.

- Complex, multi-entity, multi-scenario planning.
- Strong need for auditability, governance, and ESG.
- Desire to modernize beyond legacy cubes and spreadsheets.
